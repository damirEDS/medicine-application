Sneakers and Their Types.

Running Sneakers
Running sneakers are designed specifically for runners and have special cushioning and sole for better shock absorption during each step. They can also have additional features such as ventilation, water protection, and more.
Gym Sneakers:
Gym sneakers are designed for workouts in the gym and usually have a more dense sole and more stable construction, allowing for safer and more comfortable workouts.
Basketball Sneakers
Basketball sneakers are designed to provide better support and stability for basketball players during fast and sudden movements on the court. They often have high tops for ankle support and a thicker sole for better traction.
Walking Sneakers
Walking sneakers are designed for everyday wear and casual walking. They have a more flexible sole for a natural stride, and often have extra cushioning in the heel and forefoot for added comfort during longer walks.
Trail Running Sneakers
Trail running sneakers are designed for off-road running and have a more rugged sole for better traction on uneven terrain. They often have extra protection in the toe and heel areas, and may have water-resistant or waterproof materials to protect the feet in wet conditions.
Cross-training Sneakers
Cross-training sneakers are designed for a variety of workouts and activities, including weightlifting, HIIT, and cardio exercises. They have a flat and stable sole for better balance during weightlifting, and often have added cushioning for impact protection during high-intensity exercises.
